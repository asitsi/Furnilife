Yoga for Back Pain

Choose Gain over your Back Pain!

Lusis Monteiro Design Director

Back pain has been one of the most common reasons, especially in India, to visit a doctor. Back pain usually arises when an individual is suffering from Arthritis or Osteoporosis. Also, in extremely common cases, back bain pain is usually a result of poor physical posture. It might even be the result of the repeated heavy lifting (gym, much? Stop) or disk disease. Indian Journal of Pain recently brought out a graph where it stated that - "The occurrence of lower back pain in India is alarming with nearly 60% of the people in India suffering from lower back pain at some point of time in their lifespan." It is notably alarming as young adults ranging from 16-20 years of age make up 20% of the total population who go for back pain treatment.

Are you worried about what can be a remedy for back pain? Can rest be one? (NO. It can rather worsen it, rather). The most effective treatment for back pain is yoga. Yoga can greatly be effective in reducing back pain and in maintaining a robust spine. Yoga can stretch as well as increase and improve important back muscles. It can bring remarkable improvement in body alignment, so that body can become flexible. To burst your myth, yoga isn't just about turning and bending your body, it is about achieving a proper balance in body posture. In fact, a study on the effect of Iyengar yoga (a type of yoga) therapy for chronic low back pain showed that patients experienced less back pain after doing Iyengar yoga for at least an hour and a half, every week for 16 weeks and at-home practice for 30 minutes 5 days a week.
